## Design Context

### Users
Non-technical Japanese staff at ツナガル株式会社 — primarily office workers (not engineers) adopting Claude as a productivity tool. The typical reader has high enthusiasm but low technical familiarity with AI. The immediate audience for v0.1 is a manager-level stakeholder. They read this document to understand what Claude is, how to use it safely, and what concrete tasks it can help with. They need to feel confident and informed, not overwhelmed.

### Brand Personality
**安心・実直・構造的** — Trustworthy, straightforward, structured.

The document must not look like a marketing brochure, a technical spec, or a trendy startup product page. It should feel like a well-made internal handbook: serious but not cold, clear but not dumbed-down, professional but human.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Professional and structured. Clear hierarchy, deliberate whitespace, quiet typography.
- **Color:** Neutral — white background, near-black text (#1A1A1A), grey accents (#4A4A4A). No brand color yet. Risk levels use emoji only (✅ ⚠️ 🚫), not colored backgrounds.
- **References:** Notion docs, Apple product manuals, Stripe documentation. Clean section markers, left-accent bars on headings.
- **Anti-references:** Flashy dashboards, startup landing pages, heavy gradients, decorative icons, anything that looks like an ad.
- **Theme:** Light mode only.
- **Typography:** System font stack with Noto Sans JP for Japanese support. Body 16px, line-height 1.75. Large heading scale.
- **Layout:** Centered content max-width 800px, sticky left sidebar navigation on desktop, collapsible on mobile.

### Design Principles
1. **Hierarchy over decoration** — Visual structure communicates importance. Every size, weight, and spacing decision should serve navigation, not aesthetics.
2. **Typography is the design** — No icons, no illustrations, no color fills. The quality of the layout comes from spacing, weight contrast, and alignment.
3. **Callouts earn their place** — Blockquotes and warning blocks are used sparingly and only when a reader genuinely needs to stop and pay attention.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Sections feel self-contained. A reader jumping to §3 should not feel lost.
5. **Print-ready** — The HTML should degrade gracefully to PDF via browser print, with proper page breaks and no navigation chrome.
